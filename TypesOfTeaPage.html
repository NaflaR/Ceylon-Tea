    "Nothing soothes your throat like a hot cup of tea."
    Discovering many different types of flavours of tea, you enjoy the delightful experience of its own.
    From Rich and Bold Black teas to Light and Fruity White teas. There are so many different types that it's impossible not to find the one that suits your taste.


    Types Of Teas Tea Images Description
    Black Tea

    The most flavorful of teas, black teas are oxidized for longer
    than their green and white variants, producing deeper aromas
    and strong tastes. Expect full-bodied, rich flavours and shades
    from black to dark red.


    Stated Benefits: Several studies have found that black tea
    can decrease the risk of stroke, stablize cholestoerol
    and lower blood pressure.


    Caffeine, yes? Yeah, but normally a bit less
    than a cup of coffee.

    Green Tea

    This traditional Chinese tea is made from leaves that are not
    oxidized as long as black tea leaves, creating flavors that vary
    from earthy to toasty to seaweedy. However, it might look yellowish.


    Stated Benefits: Higher appetite, reduced cholestoerol, and
    likely lower risks of heart disease.


    Caffeine, yes? Yes, around half a cup of coffee.

    White Tea

    It is produced from the same plant that produces black and green tea,
    but although black tea is fully oxidized and green tea is not oxidized,
    white tea is semi-oxidized and then rolled to give its signature shape.
    White tea could be greenish or brownish in colour, and there are large
    variations in taste.


    Stated Benefits: Less studied than black or green tea, but some
    tests have found that white tea can help minimize the risk of diabetes.
    Plus, it comes with a number of antioxidants.


    Caffeine, yes? Yes, but generally less than black tea.
